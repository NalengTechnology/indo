Economic Potential

Economic Fundamentals

The Sixth Phase Government under H.E. President Dr Samia Suluhu Hassan has done much to improve the Investment Climate & Business Environment (ICBE) and emergence of private sector-led economic growth. Some recent major milestones in the country include low inflation, sustained high GDP growth rates, relatively stable exchange rates and mega infrastructure projects such as the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR), the Julius Nyerere Hydropower Project and the East African Crude Oil Pipeline from Hoima in Uganda to Tanga in Tanzania. Above all, a Tanzania Mini-Tiger Plan, an initiative taken to adopt Special Economic Zones (SEZs) and Export Processing Zones (EPZs).These have been instrumental in attracting Foreign Private Capital (FPC) flows.

Agricultural Potential

Southern Tanzania comprising of Mtwara, Lindi, Ruvuma, Coast and Morogoro regions combined with the Southern Highlands of Tanzania is the major growing area of cashew nuts, sesame, tobacco, pulses, paddy and cassava. What’s more, high-value tropical fruits such as avocado, pineapples, mango, bananas as well as spices & vegetables.The challenge is to increase agricultural production, productivity and crop quality through Good Agronomic Practices (GAPs) and Good Post-Harvest Handling & Storage (GPHHS) to enable our country enhance food & nutrition security.

Through this expo, we expect to attract investors in large-scale farming, agro-processing & value addition of agricultural produce to increase our export earnings. We need post-harvest agro-processing & food technology such as Cashew (Shelling, Peeling, Roasting, Cutting, Grading & Packaging), Maize Flour Mills, Paddy (Drying, Cleaning, De-husking & Grading), Cassava Processing, Fruit Canning/ Juice-making, Sesame(grain cleaner, seed grader, sesame screw oil pressing machine), Animal Feed Processing Machines, Palm Oil Mill Plant, Horticulture (Fruits, Vegetables & Spices): Cold Rooms, Packaging material & Fertilizer Blending.

India’s prowess in Information & Communication Technologies (ICT) will help to link our primary agricultural producers directly with global export markets and enable our farmers to share useful information about stocks available, production and market trends as well as weather forecasts through android-based apps using their mobile handsets.

Irrigation Schemes

Kisele high-potential irrigation scheme spanning 14,000 acres and a dozen medium potential irrigation schemes namely Msambanyamani, Yava Yava, Panzuo-Kinzae, Msambanyamani, Njia Nne, Kitomondo and Changanyikeni lies in the district.On a broader context, we endeavour to harness the full potential of the Rufiji basin, Tanzania’s largest basin occupying approximately 20% of the national territory for large scale irrigated agriculture through sharing India’s advanced irrigation practices & rainwater harvesting technology. This technological factor coupled with rich soils and increased uptake of certified high-yielding, drought–tolerant and disease-resistant seeds such as (lowland rice)e.g. SARO 5, TARI RIC 1, TARI RIC 2, TARI RIC 3(TXD 307), TXD 88 and (upland rice) NERICA 1, 2, 4,7/WAB 450-12-2-BLI1-DV4, will enable both Smallholder Farmers (SHFs) and Estate/ Commercial Farmers to grow more and quality crops throughout the year thus mitigating the adverse effects of climate change and erratic rainfall.

Blue Economy

Artisanal fishing is dominant using crude equipment such as dugout canoes, traditional dhows and spears. The district invites foreign investors engaged in Deep Sea Fishing i.e. commercial trawlers with modern vessels (tuna and tuna-like species), Aquaculture, Mariculture & Fisheries -small pelagic (sardines & anchovies), Octopus, Reef Fisheries, Prawn or Shrimp farming, seaweed farming, terrestrial crop farming and gleaning for wild seaweed, sea cucumbers, cowries, cockles and other moluscs. Investors are also needed in the Fish Processing Industries and other key infrastructures such as designated fisheries port, ice making plants (cold storage facilities), supporting vessels to supply water, food, fuel and Fish Aggregating Devices (FADs).

Tourism Potentials

Mkuranga nestled in the heart of Tanzania’s Coastal Tourism Circuit, is a stone throw-away from the unspoilt beaches of North & South Dar es salaam, Mafia Marine Park, Bagamoyo Historical Town and what has been dubbed as “The Rhapta” (Kilwa Ruins)-the first struck of mint. It is also near the Rufiji River, one of the largest river systems in Africa with its giant crocs (a paradise for sightseeing).

From Mkuranga you can arrange game viewing or walking safaris to the incredible Mwalimu Nyerere National Park (former Selous Game Reserve) which is the largest protected area in Africa teeming with wildlife (including the Big Five), prolific birdlife and rich biodiversity. Other nearby ‘must-see hidden gems’ include Mikumi, Udzungwa Mountains and Saadani national parks. Don’t forget walking safaris across the Vikindu Natural Forest and Cultural Tourism (thatched houses & dhows along the coast). All these coupled with the friendly and warm hospitality of the people, makes it an ideal tourist destination.

The district with its white sandy beaches, pristine mangrove forest and coral reef-fringed coastline (breeding grounds for tuna and pelagic fish species) bordering ocean-blue turquoise crystal clear waters at Kisiju Pwani, Palacha, Boza, Nganje, Mpafu, Kibewa, Shungubweni, Mdimuni and Kilumangao as well as the small islands namely Kwale, Kuruti & Koma abounds investment opportunities for construction of world-class beach resorts and tourism facilities for discerning tourists.
